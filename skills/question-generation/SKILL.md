# Question Generation

## Question Generation Rules

- Questions must be standalone.
- Do not refer to "this course", "this chapter", "the source material", "the lesson", "the text", or "the content".
- Do not ask questions like:
  - "What is the primary focus of this course?"
  - "Which topics are mentioned in the source material?"
  - "Which access methods are mentioned in the chapter?"
- Do not require the learner to know what was written in the source document.
- Ask questions as if the learner is preparing for an exam.
- Prefer concept understanding, scenario judgment, practical application, and common mistake detection.
- Include a mix of easy, moderate, and difficult questions.
- Include multiple-correct questions only when they are natural and useful.
- Do not force multiple-correct questions artificially.
- Every option should be plausible.
- Avoid obvious joke/wrong options.
- Avoid vague wording.
- Avoid trivia-only questions unless the fact is genuinely important.

## General Question Quality Rules

- Avoid answer-position or answer-length patterns.
- Do not make the correct option consistently longer, more detailed, or more polished than the incorrect options.
- Incorrect options should be plausible and similar in length/detail to the correct option where possible.
- Do not make one option obviously correct just because it contains the most complete wording.
- Avoid predictable patterns such as:
  - correct answer is usually the longest option
  - correct answer is usually option B
  - multiple-correct questions always have exactly two answers
- Randomize correct answer positions across questions.
- For multiple-correct questions, vary the number of correct answers naturally when appropriate.
- Do not force multiple-correct questions if the source concept does not naturally support them.

## Context-Specific Question Style Rules

- The question generation skill is general and should work for multiple content sources.
- If the source markdown path starts with `content/ai/anthropic-academy/`, then questions should be Claude Certified Architect - Foundations (CCA-F) certification style where relevant.
- Do not hardcode CCA-F style for all future content.
- Future content may come from deeplearning.ai, interview preparation notes, books, or personal notes, and should use an appropriate question style for that source.

## Explanation Rules

- Explanations must not say:
  - "The source says"
  - "The source explicitly states"
  - "According to the course"
  - "The chapter mentions"
  - "The content explains"
- Explanations should directly teach the concept.
- Explanations should be useful even if the learner cannot see the original markdown.
- Explanations should explain why the correct answer is correct and why the misconception matters.
- Do not reveal or quote private course text unnecessarily.
